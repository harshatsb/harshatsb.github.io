I'm convinced that one does not rise to the occasion when it comes calling for the first time. No matter the number of times I play the imaginary piano in font of me, or visualize my arguments on the brief listed for tomorrow, come judgement day-it all falls apart. My imagination, or lack thereof, seems to be a distrustful servant; like Aurelius but the slave whispers only falsehood. The only consistency, thus, seems to be - it didn't work out. Perhaps, it doesn't work out because it's not supposed to. To make it work is the anomaly. Perhaps, that is exactly why we tend to remember the slight appeasement of good in a week of terrible. It would be juvenile of me to assume that it is the "work outs" that have enabled this budding writer. Perhaps, I only write because I have no other plans. Does anyone? I ask. I take from Kipling, triumph and disaster are two imposters. I take from Nolan, victory has defeated you. Celebrations are just as deafening as the sounds of silence. Even more troublesome, is that when it "works out", one may not feel the urge to bet again. For they are intelligent who know the limitations of luck. However, there's no bias in what does not work out. Its equally blind to the fresh litigator as it is to the one wearing the gown of seniority. It is not, however, blind to the crows feet and worn out tools and shiny shoes. What other plans do you have? If not to drag yourself and take the path again. Perhaps, another one that leads to nowhere and doesn't work out. Perchance, if we don't have any other plans, we'll find a view scenic enough before we eventually turn back.
